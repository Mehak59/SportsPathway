Taekwondo

Taekwondo is a martial art that originated in Korea. It is known for its dynamic and powerful kicking techniques, as well as its focus on self-defense, discipline, and sportsmanship. Taekwondo practitioners train to improve their physical fitness, coordination, and mental focus.
The art emphasizes the use of hands and feet to deliver strikes, blocks, and kicks. Taekwondo competitions often feature sparring matches, where participants use controlled strikes to score points.

WHAT IS TAEKWONDO?

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art and combat sport that combines punching and kicking techniques with a focus on striking, kicking, and jumping. The word Taekwondo can be translated as tae ("strike with foot"), kwon ("strike with hand"), and do ("the art or way").

In addition to its five tenets of courtesy, integrity, perseverance, self-control and indomitable spirit, the sport requires three physical skills: poomsae (품새), kyorugi (겨루기) and gyeokpa (격파).

EQUIPMENT USED

Dobok: A traditional uniform consisting of a white jacket, pants, and belt, made of heavy cotton.

Hugo (Chest Guard): A protective gear worn to prevent injuries to the ribs, heart, and chest from kicks.

Shin Guards: Essential equipment to prevent injuries to the legs, made of dense materials that reduce the intensity of blows.

Headgear: A padded helmet that covers both ears, protecting fighters from head or ear injuries.

Groin Pads and Cup: Recommended protective gear, although it's forbidden to intentionally target this area in Taekwondo.

Mat: A thick, sturdy mat (at least one inch thick) is recommended for training and competition to provide stability and reduce the risk of falls.

TAEKWONDO COURT

The mat itself is square-shaped, with a minimum size of 10 meters by 10 meters (33 feet by 33 feet) and a maximum size of 12 meters by 12 meters (39 feet by 39 feet).

The octagonal combat area within the center of the mat is approximately 8 meters (26 feet) in diameter, with each side of the octagon having a length of approximately 3.3 meters (11 feet).

HOW TO PLAY TAEKWONDO?

Match Structure: Each match consists of three rounds, each lasting 2 minutes. There is a 1-minute break between rounds. The match is won by the athlete with the most points at the end of the three rounds. If both athletes are tied, a Golden Point Round (GPR) is fought. The first athlete to score in the GPR wins the match.

Etiquette: Competitors must bow to the judges and each other before and after the match.

Prohibited Acts: Attacking an opponent's face or below the waist. Using the knee or head to block or kick an opponent. Holding or grabbing an opponent's uniform or equipment. Deliberately touching the floor with any part of the body other than the sole of the foot.

Penalties: A gam-jeom is declared when an athlete commits a prohibited act. If an athlete accumulates 5 gam-jeoms in a single round, their opponent is automatically declared the winner of that round. If an athlete accumulates 10 gam-jeoms throughout the match, they are disqualified.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

1. One point for a valid punch to the trunk protector

2. Two Points for a valid kick to the trunk protector

3. Four points for a valid turning kick to the trunk protector

4. Three points for a valid kick to the head

5. Five points for a valid turning kick to the head

6. One point awarded for every penalty (known as gam-jeom) given against the opponent

INDIANS IN TAEKWONDO

Latika Bhandari:
Latika Bhandari is an Indian taekwondo player born on March 8, 1994, in Nainital, India. She is a 30-year-old athlete who has made a significant impact in the sport. With a height of 166 cm (5 ft 5 in) and a weight of 56 kg (123 lb), Latika has achieved numerous accolades, including being the top-ranked female taekwondo player in India as of June 2021. She has won multiple national championships, including two times National Games of India champion, and has participated in three consecutive Asian Games (2010, 2014, and 2018). Latika has also received the prestigious Vikram Award, Madhya Pradesh's Highest Sports award, in 2012. Her dedication and success have contributed to promoting taekwondo in India, making her a notable figure in the country's sports landscape.

MAJOR TAEKWONDO TOURNAMENTS

The most major notable Tournaments in TAEKWONDO are:
Olympic Games: Held every 4 years, featuring the best athletes from around the world, with gold, silver, and bronze medals awarded.

World Taekwondo Championship: Held every two years by World Taekwondo (WT), this is the most prestigious and widely recognized international taekwondo tournament.

ITF Taekwon-Do World Championships: Organized by the International Taekwon-Do Federation (ITF), this tournament features competitions in kyorugi and poomsae.

World Cup Taekwondo Championships: Although not as widely recognized as the World Championships, the World Cup is another significant international tournament organized by World Taekwondo (WT).
