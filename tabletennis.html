Table Tennis

Table tennis, also known as ping pong, is a fast-paced sport that involves two or four players hitting a lightweight ball back and forth across a table using small paddles. The objective is to score points by making the ball land on the opponent's side of the table in a way that they cannot return it. It requires quick reflexes, strategic thinking, and precision.

WHAT IS TABLE TENNIS?

Table tennis is a popular indoor sport that originated in England during the late 19th century. It is played on a rectangular table divided by a net, with players using paddles to hit the ball. The game can be played in singles or doubles format and is recognized for its competitive nature and Olympic status.

EQUIPMENT USED

Paddle: A table tennis paddle is made of a wooden blade covered with rubber on one or both sides, allowing players to strike the ball effectively.
Ball: The ball used in table tennis is lightweight, typically made of plastic, and measures 40mm in diameter. It is designed to be durable and responsive to strikes.

TABLE TENNIS TABLE

A standard table tennis table measures 2.74 meters long and 1.525 meters wide, with a height of 76 centimeters. The table is divided into two equal halves by a net, and it must be flat and uniform in surface for fair play.

HOW TO PLAY TABLE TENNIS?

The game starts with one player serving the ball. Players take turns hitting the ball over the net, aiming to land it on the opponent's side of the table. Points are scored when an opponent fails to return the ball correctly, or when the ball hits the table and is unreturnable.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

Points are scored in table tennis when the opponent fails to return the ball properly, either by missing the ball, hitting it out of bounds, or failing to make the ball bounce on their side of the table. Matches are typically played to 11 or 21 points, depending on the rules set before the game.

SERVING IN TABLE TENNIS

The server must throw the ball vertically upwards at least 16cm and strike it behind the end line of the table. The ball must first bounce on the server's side before crossing the net and landing on the opponent's side.

INDIANS IN TABLE TENNIS

India has made significant strides in table tennis, producing world-class players such as Sharath Kamal and Manika Batra. The sport is gaining popularity across the country, with various tournaments held to promote talent and engagement at all levels.

MAJOR TABLE TENNIS TOURNAMENTS

1. World Table Tennis Championships: This prestigious event is held every two years, featuring the best players globally.
2. Olympic Games: Table tennis has been part of the Summer Olympics since 1988, with events for both men and women.
3. ITTF World Tour: An annual series of competitions that attracts top players from around the world.
4. Commonwealth Games: Table tennis is featured in this multi-sport event, showcasing athletes from Commonwealth nations.
