Hammer Throw

The hammer throw is an athletic event that involves throwing a heavy metal ball attached to a wire handle. The athlete swings the hammer in a circular motion around their head and body, building up momentum before releasing it. The goal is to throw the hammer as far as possible. The hammer throw is a complex and physically demanding event that requires strength, power, coordination, and technique. It is a popular event in track and field competitions around the world.

WHAT IS HAMMER THROW?

The hammer throw is one of the four throwing events in regular outdoor track and field competitions, along with the discus throw, shot put and javelin.
The "hammer" used in this sport is not like any of the tools also called by that name. It consists of a metal ball attached by a steel wire to a grip. These three components are each separate and can move independently. Both the size and weight of the ball vary between men's and women's events.

EQUIPMENT USED

Hammer: A Hammer used in this sport is equipment with a steel wire adjoining to a metal ball at one end and a handle at another. The metal head should be of solid iron, brass or any other metal but not softer than brass.

Gloves: Throwers wear leather gloves eith thick fingers to ensure a solid grip on the handle.

Clothing: Throwers wear lightweight vests and shorts. The shorts may be tight fitted to support the thighs.

Shoes: Shoes with no cleats are worn by throwers to allow maximum adherence and to make the rotation easier.

HOW TO HAMMER THROW?

Priliminary Swinging: Use two swinging motions to generate momentum. The swinging must start from behind the right side of your body, taking the hammer in the forward direction away from your body. The low point of the hammer will be in a 12 o'clock position, and the tip will be in a 6 o'clock position with respect to your body.

Turning Phase: After the swinging, enter the turning phase. The hammer will come directly in front of you, and you'll start rotating the low point. Through repetitive rotation, the ball will achieve acceleration, while you keep rotating with your feet in contact with the circle.

Release: Release the hammer at the high point, opposite the low point. This is typically around the 3 o'clock to 12 o'clock position.

Follow Through: Complete the remaining turns (3 o'clock to 12 o'clock) using the ball of your foot. Shift your left foot to the heel level as the hammer approaches the 12 o'clock position, and initiate the next turn.

HOW ARE POINTS SCORED?

Measurement: The distance a hammer is thrown is measured by a judge or multiple judges upon a successful attempt. This distance is then recorded as a score.

Ranking: Athletes are ranked according to their best throw, with the farthest throw receiving the highest ranking.

Competition Format: In most competitions, including the Olympics, athletes have six total attempts to achieve their best throw. The athlete with the furthest distance after six attempts is crowned the winner.

SCORING SYSTEM

The scoring system in hammer throw is based on the distance the hammer travels. The athlete who throws the hammer the farthest distance wins the competition.

Ranking: The athletes are ranked based on the distance of their best throw.

Ties: If two or more athletes have the same best throw, a tiebreaker is used. This can include re-throws or other criteria determined by the competition rules.

INDIANS IN HAMMER THROW

Praveen Sobti : Considered India's first and only hammer throw medalist in the Commonwealth Games, Sobti won a silver medal in 1966. He was a former Border Security Force (BSF) soldier and also won four medals, including two golds, in the Asian Games for India.

Ashish Jhakar: In 2018, Jhakar set a new national record in the U20 men's hammer throw event at the Junior Federation Cup.

MAJOR HAMMER THROW TOURNAMENTS

The most prestigious Tournaments in Hammer Throw are:
Olympic Games: Held every 4 years, featuring the best athletes from around the world, with gold, silver, and bronze medals awarded.

World Championships: Biennial event, organized by the International Association of Athletics Federations (IAAF), featuring top athletes competing for gold, silver, and bronze medals.

European Championships: Held every 2 years, featuring top European athletes competing for gold, silver, and bronze medals.

IAAF World Indoor Championships: Biennial event, featuring indoor hammer throw competitions for athletes from around the world.
